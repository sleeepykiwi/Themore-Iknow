Curiosities

At the suggestion of Tame Impala’s Kevin Parker, the illustrator Beatty based Currents cover on a phenomenon known as “vortex shedding”. In fluid dynamics, vortex shedding occurs when an object disrupts the flow of air or water, like the eddies that form when a river passes around a rock. But we do not have a “real life” depiction of vortex shedding on the cover. Beatty’s abstraction, his choice to not represent familiar objects or scenes, orients us to thinking about the album as an experience of form rather than, say, as a story or scene.

Syneonline.com

Curiosities

In the 1910s, Kolmanskop was one of the richest towns in the world thanks to its diamond mines. By the 1930s, Kolmanskop’s diamond supply was running low, so its residents packed up and headed south. Now, the place is a ghost town, whom buildings are engulfed by sand dunes. This town provided the inspiration for the cover art for Tame Impala’s album, The Slow Rush. Photographer Neil Krug travelled to Kolmanskop with Kevin Parker last year, shooting a series of images in buildings around the town and editing them to create surreal interiors in vivid blues and reds. The photos perfectly capture the dreamy, psychedelic tone of Parker’s music.

Neilkrug.com

Curiosities

Kevin Parker is of mixed ethnicity, as his father was from Zimbabwe and his mother is from South Africa. He learned music from his father, who practiced music as a hobby. His father was also part of a cover band that performed songs of various popular bands, such as ‘Supertramp,’ ‘The Beach Boys,’ and ‘The Beatles.’ Parker’s first experience of playing an instrument was when he accompanied his father on the guitar. His father believed that music would not be fun and mysterious to those who try to make a career out of it. Parker paid attention to his father’s advice and enrolled himself at a university to study engineering. Meanwhile, he had created a band called ‘The Dee Dee Dums’ along with Dominic Simper and Luke Epstein.

Thefamouspeople.com
